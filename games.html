Ship It!

A game with short rounds where you quickly sort items. Items come down a conveyor belt quickly and the player must sort as many items before the time runs out. I built the difficulty curve around three of Garneau's forms of fun: application of skill, competition and completion. Every tuning decision in the GDD ties back to one of them.

Genre: Arcade, time attack

Engine: Unity

Team: Solo

My roles: Game Designer, Programmer, Artist

Level Design

A level design log for a 2D wall-jump platformer with five levels. Each level is built to teach a new mechanic without tutorial prompts. A level introduces one idea, tests it on its own, then mixes it with what came before. I greybox every level before any art goes in, and I write up the layout decisions as I make them. The page grows as the project does.

Genre: 2D platformer

Engine: Unity

Team: Solo

Hop Hop Hop!

An arcade survival game where the water is the enemy. Lilypads sink under the frog and break quickly, so no spot is safe for long. The frog's tongue only fires the way the frog is facing which makes catching flies about positioning and not reflexes. The player is trying to survive as long as possible and depending on how long they last they may earn a bronze, silver or gold tier.

Genre:Survival

Engine: Unity

Team: Solo

My roles: Game Designer, Programmer, Artist

Smooth Snake

A game for the Nintendo DS that can run on real hardware. The snake follows the stylus smoothly instead of being locked to a grid, which changes the whole feel of the game. The score display is drawn pixel by pixel in C, with no image files at all and a persistent save state keeps your best score between play sessions.

Genre: Arcade

Platform: Nintendo DS

Team: Solo

My roles: Game Designer, Programmer, Artist

Sky High

A physics stacking game where thin pieces score more and topple easier. If you want a high score every placement is a bet. I tuned the shapes and scoring so the safe moves aren't as rewarding as risky ones and the tower falling always feels like the player's responsibility.

Genre: Physics, arcade

Engine: Unity

Team: Solo

My roles: Game Designer, Programmer

About

I'm a game designer who comes from a software development background and have a lifelong love of games. I care about how a game feels to play as much as how it works underneath, and I can build what I design: Unity, C, Python, JavaScript and Kotlin are just some of my skills.

I care about why games are fun and the design choices that make them that way. This is what I want to do for a living. Every project here was made in my own time because I love this craft.

I hold EU and US citizenship and I'm open to relocating for the right role.
