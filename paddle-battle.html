A local two-player arcade game built in Java where two players fight to get the ball past each other.

The full design report for this project including the design pillars, audience, core gameplay breakdown, and pacing decisions is available as a download further down this page.

Design

I built Paddle Battle mainly to get comfortable with object-oriented principles in Java rather than to reinvent Pong itself.

The design pillars I kept coming back to were competition, application of a skill, and social interaction. The game's fun is how its played as two people need to be in the same room as they play. I built it strictly for local multiplayer. The tension of a rally is best when there's someone right there to experience it with you.
