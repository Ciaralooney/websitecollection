A platformer game built in Java using Greenfoot, made as a group project during my time at St Johns. The game has three levels each with a distinct theme. It was one of my first experiences working in a team creating a video game.

What I worked on

I programmed and designed Level 2 from end to end.

  • Implemented the core player movement and platformer physics (acceleration, platform collision, gravity)
  • Level Design for Level 2. Choosing the placement of platforms and coins
  • Designed the background and platform assets for level 2. These were made in Photoshop.
  • Implemented the coin score counter
  • Initially implemented a timer system, then iterated on this based on group decisions
  • Added the Level 2 title screen and the level reset on fall mechanic

We worked as a group of three, completing most of the work during labs with a weekly meeting to make design decisions and check in on progress.

Design decisions

We decided early on to drop the timer mechanic and go with a level reset on fall instead of a lives system. Resetting the level on a fall keeps the game moving without a game over screen interrupting the flow, and felt more forgiving for a casual platformer.

There is one thing I would change if I revisited the project. Holding the space bar lets the ghost character float to the top of the level, which makes it too easy. It was something we noticed late in development and did not get the chance to fix. That said, it ended up being a fun little quirk to mess around with while playing.

Feedback

The feedback we received was very positive. Our game was rated 4 stars overall and people mentioned things such as great character and level design, enjoyable game play and the winning screen being a nice touch
