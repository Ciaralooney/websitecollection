Smooth Snake

A snake game for the Nintendo DS inspired by the Snake Game by OneMotion, a Flash game I used to play growing up. When Flash was discontinued the original went offline, so I made my own version I could play again. The snake follows your mouse rather than moving in a grid, which is the mechanic that always made the original feel different from other snake games.

The full design report for this project, including the design pillars, audience, core gameplay breakdown, and pacing decisions, is available as a download further down this page.

Design

Working within the limitations of the DS hardware meant I had to think carefully about which parts of the original game were essential to the feel of it. The mouse controlled movement was non-negotiable. That's what made the original distinct. Everything else was secondary to keeping that intact. I knew this would translate well into a DS port using the stylus and touchscreen.

There are two things missing compared to the original: background music and obstacles. I wasn't able to figure out audio for a first DS game, and obstacles would have needed more experience with the platform to implement well. Honestly neither of them takes away from the gameplay. I made this for myself and I'm very happy to have a version of this game I can play easily once again.

You can download it here if you want to try it.
