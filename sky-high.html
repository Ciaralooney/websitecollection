Sky High

A 3D physics stacking game built in Unity. You can drag shapes and drop them to try build a stack as high as possible before it topples. Different shapes carry different risk and reward. Large and wide shapes are safer but score less points while thin unstable shapes are worth far more if you can stack them.

The full design report for this project, including the design pillars, audience, core gameplay breakdown and pacing decisions is available as a download further down this page.

Design

I wanted to reward players who took risks so thinner pieces that are harder to stack will net players more points than safer larger pieces. Choosing a variety of pieces is also rewarded as the first time you choose a new shape you will get points that are multiplied by the amount of unique shapes you have. I worked carefully on the scoring system so the game rewards risk instead of building a tower with one shape.

Controls: The player chooses a shape form the tray. They drag the shape out and can choose the horizontal placement before dropping the piece, this felt more suited as the game isn't about speed its about deliberate choices.
