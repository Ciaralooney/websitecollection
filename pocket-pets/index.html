A Tamagotchi-style virtual pet app for Android built in Kotlin. The assignment brief was to make a booking app demonstrating the use of a Room database but once I read this I figured I could meet the brief with something more interesting. I pitched adopting and releasing pets as the booking and cancellation mechanic, got it approved, and built from there. It knew it would be more work than a straightforward booking app but a lot more fun to make and was more rewarding. This project received a grade of 83%

The full design report for this project, including the design pillars, audience, core gameplay breakdown, and pacing decisions, is available as a download further down this page.

Design

The core loop is adoption, care, and release. From the adoption screen you can browse available pets and adopt any you like. This will update its status in the database. Your adopted pets live on the My Pets screen, each showing their current hunger and happiness levels. Tapping a pet brings you to the care screen where you can feed it, play with it, and clean it. Each pet's stats decrease over time so there's always something to do.

The feature I'm most happy with is the proximity sensor interaction. When you tap the heart icon on the care screen and bring your device close to your body, the sensor detects the movement and the pet jumps, gaining happiness. It felt like a natural fit for a pet care app and gave the interaction a physical quality that a button tap wouldn't have.

The app uses ViewModel on each screen to manage UI state and avoid unnecessary database queries, and the proximity sensor is only activated when the user triggers it deliberately, so it doesn't drain battery in the background.
