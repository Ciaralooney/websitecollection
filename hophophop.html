A game made in Unity. You play as a frog hopping around lilypads. Your goal is to last as long as you can without falling into the water. You can get more lives by eating flies.

The full design report for this project, including the design pillars, audience, core gameplay breakdown, and pacing decisions, is available as a download further down this page.

Design

As I made this game the main design problem I had was that nothing pushed the player forward. An early version counted how many lilypads you hopped but you could play forever with no pressure and nothing reason to continue. The fix was making the pads themselves the threat so now every pad you land on starts breaking. At the start of the game you have 3 seconds to leave before the pad sinks under you. As the game goes on this shrinks to 1.2 seconds. The early game teaches you the controls and the late game tests how you hold up when there's less time to react.

Pads sink behind you when you hop off them so you can't hop back and forth between two indefinitely. Lilypads will respawn after a while so the player doesn't end up stuck. By catching a fly with the tongue the player earns a life back so when you see a fly it's always worth hunting them down. Depending on how long you lasted it's possible to be placed in a bronze/silver/gold tier. This gives a player motivation to try again to get just 1 tier better.
